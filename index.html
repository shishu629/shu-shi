01 / About

From fundamental physics
to functional devices.

I am a Postdoctoral Research Fellow at the National University of Singapore. I received my PhD in Materials Science & Engineering from NUS in 2024, with a perfect 5.00/5.00 GPA.

My research centers on ferroelectric hafnia, epitaxial oxide thin films, resistive switching, memristors, and neuromorphic computing. I am especially interested in how interfaces and charge transfer can stabilize new material states and unlock high-performance memory.

02 / Research

Selected research directions

01

Ferroelectric Hafnia

Interfacial and charge-transfer engineering of epitaxial HfO2-based thin films, including HfZrO2/HfLaO2 multilayers.

02

Emerging Memory

Resistive switching and crystalline memristors with controlled filament growth for highly linear, precise memory synapses.

03

Neuromorphic Devices

Materials and device architectures for energy-efficient synapses, spiking neural networks, and brain-inspired computing.
