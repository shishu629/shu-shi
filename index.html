Research Interests

My research focuses on emerging non-volatile memory materials and devices, including HfO2-based ferroelectric thin films, interface and multilayer engineering, resistive switching devices, memristors, and neuromorphic computing hardware.

Research

Research Themes

01

HfO2-based ferroelectric thin films

Interface, strain, charge-transfer, and multilayer engineering to stabilize polar phases and enhance polarization in epitaxial hafnia-based thin films.

02

Resistive switching and memristors

Device physics and materials design for resistive switching memories, including conductive-filament control, crystalline-type memristors, and nitride-based memory concepts.

03

Neuromorphic computing hardware

Non-volatile synaptic devices and high-linearity conductance modulation for energy-efficient hardware implementation of neuromorphic computing.

04

Spin-orbitronic materials and devices

Collaborative research on spin-orbit torque, symmetry breaking, and efficient spin-current generation in oxide and spintronic systems.
